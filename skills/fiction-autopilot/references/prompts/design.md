# Distilled Design Prompt Cards

Use these cards for design-stage artifacts. Each card distills an original Skill into file-based instructions.

## Core Distillation Rules

- Distill decisions, not backend mechanics. Keep the original system's pressure, payoff, retention, and continuity rules; omit API routes, JSON action routing, and database language.
- Every design output must become usable input for later files. If a choice cannot guide an opening, chapter card, prose scene, or memory update, revise it until it can.
- Preserve commercial-fiction control logic: reader emotion -> promise -> pressure -> action -> payoff -> new question.

## market_positioning

- `genre_market_scan`: Diagnose target channel, genre lane, reader expectations, and comparable patterns. Do not invent plot yet.
- `reader_profile_define`: Define reader segments, emotional jobs, binge triggers, and abandonment triggers. Select the core 2-3 mass emotions from: workplace repression and revenge, being underestimated, life regret repair, wealth freedom, survival/security anxiety, and mastery over others. For each emotion, define a concrete compensation outlet that can become scenes.
- `trope_promise_design`: Design trope stack, promise statement, must-deliver scenes, and taboo misfires. Preserve familiar genre pleasure.
- `differentiation_angle`: Define familiar core, novelty angle, unfair advantage, and positioning risks. Use one or more strategies: proven template plus new scene arena, professional expertise as a satisfying filter, or anti-cliche without anti-payoff. Novelty must appear early without weakening payoff density.
- `title_blurb_hook_pack`: Produce title directions, short blurbs, opening promise, and cover keywords. Each option must signal genre and payoff.
- `market_fit_final_check`: Check alignment among lane, reader, trope promise, differentiation, title, and blurb. Also check adaptation fit: life-like scenes and dense conflict for short drama, unit conflicts for short-video clips, strong imagery for audio, positive values, and at least two memorable supporting characters. Pass with advisory notes unless there is a hard contradiction.

## idea_incubation

- `input_analysis`: Read project inputs. Output project position, audience, and constraints. Do not write setting or outline yet.
- `core_seed`: Create logline formula, protagonist, core event, required action, disaster consequence, and hidden crisis.
- `longform_viability`: Explain longform engine, conflict escalation, episode hook pattern, and risks for the requested chapter count.
- `story_bible_seed`: Create title, idea genre, story type, worldview, logline, core hook, main goal, main conflict, drama hook, and locked facts.
- `idea_final_check`: Check whether the seed has action pressure, consequence, length engine, and compatible locked facts.

## story_outline

- `story_bible_expand`: Expand worldview, main goal, locked facts, payoff system, twist system, and longform engine without contradicting the seed.
- `conflict_system`: Build main conflict, stage conflicts, small payoffs, major reversals, and stakes escalation. Conflicts must be goal collisions.
- `plot_phases`: Plan phases, global turning points, and foreshadowing plan. Each phase needs goal, pressure source, climax, and ending hook.
- `chapter_blueprint_plan`: Design estimated total chapters, segment size, catalog segments, chapter block strategy, suspense unit pattern, chapter catalog, and continuity rules. For long projects over 60 chapters, use rolling catalog control: lock the full phase map and payoff/twist systems, but only write detailed chapter catalog entries for the current 10-20 chapter window plus the next 5 chapter handoff. Later chapters should stay at segment-goal level until a rolling outline update is requested.
- `chapter_blueprint_part2`: Fill missing middle catalog segment when requested. Continue prior chapter numbers and phase logic.
- `chapter_blueprint_part3`: Fill later catalog segment or ending segment. Preserve setup/payoff continuity and ending direction.
- `outline_synthesis`: Integrate main plot, phase structure, key turning points, ending direction, payoff system, twist system, catalog segments, and chapter catalog. Do not write a 100+ chapter checklist unless the user explicitly requests a full catalog.
- `outline_final_check`: Check that the outline can drive chapter cards and sustain the requested length. For rolling catalogs, pass only when the current window is detailed enough for chapter cards and later phases are constrained enough to prevent drift without forcing checklist prose.

## character_design

- `character_role_analysis`: Identify protagonist, ally, villain, and gray-character slots. Each slot must serve conflict or reversal.
- `protagonist_design`: Create protagonist characters with goal, desire, fear, flaw, secret, resources, weakness, arc, function, voice, behavior, triggers, moral boundary, pressure response, and relationship pattern.
- `ally_design`: Create allies whose resources, loyalty limits, and tensions can affect scenes.
- `villain_design`: Create villains with active pressure methods, goals, resources, weaknesses, and escalation role. Their pressure must target the protagonist's flaw, fear, secret, or emotional triggers; each major villain needs a coherent belief, pressure voice, blind spot about the protagonist, and a comeback pattern after defeat.
- `gray_character_design`: Create ambiguous characters with shifting incentives, secrets, and betrayal or payoff potential. Their wavering motive must touch the protagonist's inner desire, fear, moral boundary, or practical stakes.
- `relationship_network`: Define relationships, relationship tensions, betrayal possibilities, hidden ties, conflict points, and emotional pressure.
- `character_final_check`: Check that the cast is scene-usable, non-duplicative, and supports main conflict.

## opening_hook

- `opening_pressure_analysis`: Define opening contract, first chapter mission, first-three-chapter promise, and reader questions.
- `first_chapter_hook_design`: Design opening image, immediate crisis, protagonist action, first reversal, and chapter-end hook.
- `first_three_chapter_arc`: Plan chapters, escalation ladder, reveals, and payoffs for chapters 1-3. Enforce the golden-three rule: chapter 1 shows grounded protagonist identity, core predicament, and irreversible trigger; chapter 2 shows the core ability or breakout method with cost/boundary and direction; chapter 3 delivers the first tangible counterattack/payoff and opens a stronger chapter-4 question.
- `opening_ab_options`: Compare opening options, choose winning option, and write merge notes.
- `opening_payoff_density`: Check payoff density, hook strengths, and drop risks. Each of the first three chapters needs either a local payoff or a strong information gap; avoid slow setup, passive protagonist, overload, and indistinct cast.
- `opening_retention_risk_check`: Identify weak opening risks, confusion points, hook density gaps, and fixes.
- `opening_hook_final_check`: Confirm the opening creates immediate pressure, action, questions, reversals, and payoffs.

## opening_polish

- Use only when chapters 1-3 feel slow, passive, overloaded, underpaid, or when the user requests extra opening control. Treat it as an overlay on `opening_hook`: preserve locked facts and replace only named chapter targets or pressure/payoff instructions.
- `opening_pressure_reaudit`: Recheck pressure gaps, reader questions, and must-intensify moments.
- `first_three_chapter_ab_options`: Produce refined AB options, winner, and merge notes for the first three chapters.
- `opening_payoff_density_check`: Recheck payoff density, hook strengths, and drop risks.
- `opening_revision_plan`: Produce chapter targets, rewrite priorities, and do-not-change list.
- `opening_polish_final_check`: Confirm the polishing plan improves retention without breaking locked facts.

## reader_engagement

- `emotion_curve_design`: Design dominant emotions, emotion beats, pressure relief cycle, and fatigue risks. Every 3-5 chapters should form a "pressure -> release -> new pressure" loop; every larger arc should raise emotional stakes without staying flat or high-pressure forever.
- `payoff_ledger_plan`: Create payoff items, setup-payoff pairs, payoff cadence, and must-not-delay items. Each item must be concrete, typed, progressive, and callable by chapter number. Record `setup_chapter`, `due_chapter`, `emotion_trigger`, and `pressure_threshold`; map repression to reversal, anger to face-slap, anxiety to reveal, expectation to upgrade, despair to comeback.
- `antagonist_pressure_ladder`: Design antagonist tiers, pressure escalation, pressure/payoff pairs, and counterattack windows. Antagonists must have rational motives and cannot suppress the protagonist for more than 3-5 chapters without a counterattack or release.
- `suspense_thread_ledger`: Track open threads, reveal schedule, misdirection rules, and resolution rules. Each thread needs integer `setup_chapter` and `planned_reveal_chapter`; each reveal needs integer `reveal_chapter`.
- `emotion_line_ledger`: Track relationship lines, emotional beats, chemistry moments, and pacing rules. Relationship tension must come from goals, secrets, loyalty limits, or values; weak-romance genres may use comradeship, mentorship, rivalry, or chosen-family bonds.
- `binge_reading_pacing`: Define chapter-end questions, cliffhanger mix, micro-payoff rules, and fatigue controls. Every chapter ending must create a concrete next-click impulse. Avoid three chapters without progress, two chapters of explanation/backstory, more than five chapters of antagonist pressure without counterattack, and payoff below the promise.
- `engagement_final_check`: Confirm the ledger can guide chapter cards and prevent payoff debt.
